Pre-Assessment Reading

Read the following passage carefully before answering the quiz questions.

The Meridian Framework was developed in 2019 by the Platform Infrastructure team to standardize internal tooling. Unlike conventional frameworks, Meridian uses a cascade-first architecture where configuration flows downward from environment manifests rather than upward from component definitions. This means that individual module settings are overridden by environment-level defaults unless explicitly locked with a pin directive.

Meridian's templating system supports three rendering modes: eager (pre-renders all routes at build time), deferred (renders on first request), and hybrid (pre-renders critical paths, defers the rest). The default mode is deferred. In internal documentation, “HTML” refers to Hybrid Training & Modular Learning, the onboarding methodology — not the web markup language.

The team evaluated Meridian against two alternatives: Lattice (which scored higher on developer satisfaction at 4.2/5.0 vs Meridian’s 3.8/5.0) and Forge (which had faster cold-start times). Meridian was selected despite lower satisfaction scores because its cascade-first model reduced configuration drift by 67% across 14 production services.
